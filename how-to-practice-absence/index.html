5. Ethical Good Ways

How to practice Absence

Overview

Ethical Good Ways are methods related to our Absence. They express the 6th Level of our flexibility, sensibility, sustainability, accessibility, and capability.

Mnemonic: Left Foot

Each of our fingers have been assigned with a different sub-level. They have also been associated with a color from the visible light spectrum.

The association with the Left foot has been based on our Brain Hemispheres and their predominant functionality. So, for most cases you may leave the list as is.

If you switched hands for the previous sets, it is mandatory to also change feet as your mnemonic.

Step 1. Ethical Flexibility

Ethical Good Ways may be expressed as Absence through Flexibility of Non-Detachment, Non-Perfection, Non-Imagination, Non-Imperfection, Non-Memory.

Definitions

  • Flexibility: The ability to adjust and adapt to changing circumstances or conditions.
  • Non-Detachment: The ability to remain connected and engaged with a situation or experience, even when it is difficult or uncomfortable.
  • Non-Perfection: The acceptance of imperfection as a positive characteristic and the understanding that perfection is impossible to achieve.
  • Non-Imagination: The ability to think outside the box and come up with creative solutions that are not pre-defined or predetermined.
  • Non-Imperfection: The understanding that mistakes and flaws are part of life and the willingness to accept them and learn from them.
  • Non-Memory: The ability to let go of past experiences and memories in order to move forward and create a better future.

Step 2. Ethical Sensibility

Ethical Good Ways may be expressed as Absence through Sensibility to Non-Hearing, Non-Touch, Non-Taste, Non-Vision, Non-Smell.

Definitions

  • Sensibility: The capacity to perceive, feel, or be conscious of something.
  • Non-Hearing: The capacity to be aware of and respond to sounds without needing to actually hear them.
  • Non-Touch: The capacity to be aware of and respond to physical contact without needing to actually feel it.
  • Non-Taste: The capacity to be aware of and respond to taste without needing to actually taste it.
  • Non-Vision: The capacity to be aware of and respond to visual information without needing to actually see it.
  • Non-Smell: The capacity to be aware of and respond to smells without needing to actually smell them.

Step 3. Ethical Sustainability

Ethical Good Ways may be expressed as Absence from the Sustainability of Non-Capacity, Non-Magnitude, Non-Coverage, Non-Formation, Non-Convertibility.

Definitions

  • Sustainability: The ability to support and maintain oneself without depleting natural resources.
  • Non-Capacity: The capacity to be self-sustaining without the need to overconsume resources.
  • Non-Magnitude: The capacity to be independent and self-sufficient without the need to rely on external sources.
  • Non-Coverage: The capacity to be resilient and adaptive without the need to be uniform and homogeneous.
  • Non-Formation: The capacity to be flexible and dynamic without the need for rigidity and stagnation.
  • Non-Convertibility: The capacity to be open and accepting without the need to conform or be restricted.

Step 4. Ethical Accessibility

Ethical Good Ways may be expressed as Absence from the Accessibility of Non-Courage, Non-Receptiveness, Non-Altruism, Non-Truthfulness, Non-Wisdom.

Definitions

  • Accessibility: The ability to access or use something, such as a resource or service.
  • Non-Courage: The capacity to take action without the need to be fearless.
  • Non-Receptiveness: The capacity to be open to new ideas and experiences without the need to agree or accept them.
  • Non-Altruism: The capacity to be generous and giving without the need to be selfless.
  • Non-Truthfulness: The capacity to be honest and genuine without the need to be absolute.
  • Non-Wisdom: The capacity to be wise and thoughtful without the need to be omniscient.

Step 5. Ethical Capability

Ethical Good Ways may be practiced through absence deriving from observations over the capability of Non-Ethical Conduct, Non-Ethical Effort, Non-Ethical Resolve, Non-Ethical View and Non-Ethical Livelihood.

Definitions

  • Capability: The capacity to do or achieve something.
  • Non-Ethical Conduct: The capacity to behave in an ethical manner without the need to be perfect.
  • Non-Ethical Effort: The capacity to strive for ethical behavior without the need to be infallible.
  • Non-Ethical Resolve: The capacity to persist in ethical behavior without the need for perfection.
  • Non-Ethical View: The capacity to see the world through an ethical lens without the need to be absolutely right or wrong.
  • Non-Ethical Livelihood: The capacity to live ethically without the need to be completely selfless or unselfish.

Summary

Being Ethical, through the Good Way of Absence, involves being flexible without the need for detachment, perfection, imagination, imperfection, memory. Moreover it involves being sensible without the need for hearing, touch, taste, vision, smell, sustainable without capacity, magnitude, coverage, formation, convertibility, accessible without courage, receptiveness, altruism, truthfulness, wisdom, and capable through sensibility over the existence of non-ethical conduct, non-ethical effort, non-ethical resolve, non-ethical view, and non-ethical livelihood.
